This object is a polychrome ceramic, which the Nasca almost exclusively made, meaning the vessel was decorated with three or more distinct colors. Polychrome was less common in comparable societies such as the Moche. In Nasca society, polychrome was largely ubiquitous. There are nine stylistic phases of Nasca pottery over a 700 year period, and this vessel, a double-spout bridge vessel painted with the Mythical Spotted Cat, falls into the third of the nine phases. The first 1-4 phases are often called the "Early Nasca Period", c. 1-450 CE.

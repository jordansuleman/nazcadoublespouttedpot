The object's two spouts and bridge's specific function are not fully known. Researchers and archaeologists can only speculate about the specific function of the spouts and bridge. The vessel's exact function remains uncertain, but its double spout likely helped with pouring liquids, while the bridge handle may have made it easier to tie onto animal packs or carry by hand. The design may also have symbolized the movement and circulation of water through rain, rivers, lakes, plants, and people, reflecting how Andean communities understood the interconnectedness and complexity of the world around them. The different spouts that the Nazca civilization would mold onto their pottery are definitely and significantly unique in the ancient world.
